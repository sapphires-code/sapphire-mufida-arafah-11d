About the Book: Negeri 5 Menara

Written by: Ahmad Fuadi

Buku ini menceritakan perjalanan hidup Alif, seorang anak Minang yang menempuh pendidikan di pesantren Madani. Awalnya penuh keraguan, namun perlahan Alif menemukan makna perjuangan, persahabatan, dan kekuatan impian.

"Man jadda wajada - Siapa yang bersungguh-sungguh, dia akan berhasil."

Kalimat itu menjadi semangat utama dalam buku ini. Kisah yang ditulis berdasarkan pengalaman nyata ini sangat menginspirasi, terutama bagi pelajar yang sedang mengejar mimpi mereka. Penulis berhasil menyampaikan pesan-pesan moral melalui narasi yang hidup.

Buku ini cocok dibaca oleh remaja dan orang dewasa yang sedang mencari motivasi hidup. Cerita persahabatan, perjuangan, dan cinta belajar membuatnya menyentuh dan relevan bagi pembaca di berbagai usia.

About the Book: Night Circus

Written by: Erin Morgenstern

The Night Circus is a magical fantasy novel about a mysterious circus that appears only at night, hiding a secret competition between two young magicians whose rivalry turns into love.

“The circus arrives without warning. No announcements precede it. It is simply there, when yesterday it was not.”

The Night Circus is about a mysterious black-and-white circus that appears without warning and opens only at night. Behind its beauty, the circus is the stage for a secret magical competition between two young illusionists, Celia and Marco, who are trained since childhood to compete—but not told the rules or the cost. As they grow closer, their rivalry turns into love, putting the circus and everyone in it at risk.

At its heart, the book explores fate vs. choice, love, sacrifice, and the quiet rebellion of choosing compassion over control.

About the Book: The Moby-dick

Written by: Herman Melville

Moby-Dick by Herman Melville is about obsession, fate, and humanity’s struggle against nature.

“From hell’s heart I stab at thee; for hate’s sake I spit my last breath at thee.”

The novel follows Ishmael, a sailor who joins a whaling ship called the Pequod, led by the intense and mysterious Captain Ahab. Ahab is consumed by his desire for revenge against Moby Dick, a massive white whale that previously destroyed his leg. As the voyage continues, Ahab’s obsession takes control of the entire crew, pushing them toward a dangerous and inevitable confrontation.

Beyond the whale hunt, the book explores deeper questions about meaning, madness, belief, free will, and the power of nature, using the sea as a symbol of the unknown.

About the Book: The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, The Witch and The Wardrobe

Written by: C.S Lewis

The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe by C.S. Lewis is a fantasy novel about courage, sacrifice, and the battle between good and evil.

“Once a king or queen in Narnia, always a king or queen.”.”

The story follows four siblings—Peter, Susan, Edmund, and Lucy Pevensie—who are sent to the countryside during World War II. While exploring an old wardrobe, Lucy discovers the magical land of Narnia, a world trapped in an endless winter by the White Witch.

As the children return to Narnia together, they learn of Aslan, the great lion and true ruler of the land, who is working to defeat the Witch. When Edmund betrays his siblings, Aslan makes a powerful sacrifice to save him. In the end, good triumphs over evil, the Witch is defeated, and the children help restore peace to Narnia, becoming its kings and queens.
